The Benefit Risk Assessment of VaccinEs (BRAVE) toolkit is an interactive web application that employs statistical methods to quantify the benefits and risks of vaccines.

In the current version, the method has been applied to infer benefits and risks of COVID-19 vaccines for populations of interest based on available data sources within the EU/EEA.
The toolkit models the benefits and risks that are potentially associated with COVID-19 vaccination.

Benefit outcomes

Benefits of COVID-19 vaccines are quantified as the number of prevented COVID-19 confirmed cases, hospitalisations, ICU admissions and deaths. Quantification of the benefits associated with COVID-19 vaccination is done directly by comparing the observed number of COVID-19 confirmed cases, hospitalisation, ICU admissions and deaths with the expected number that would have occurred had no COVID-19 vaccination been used.

Risk outcomes

In the toolkit, the benefits are offset against the following safety signals that were investigated by EMA as potentially being associated with a specific COVID-19 vaccine.
  • Myocarditis & pericarditis for the mRNA vaccines (Comirnaty and Spikevax), 14 days after vaccination.
  • Thrombosis with thrombocytopenia (TTS) for the adenovirus vaccine Vaxzevria, 30 days after vaccination.
The user can upload data within the application to calculate the outcomes of interest for their population/vaccine of interest. Uploaded data files can be in the ‘.rds’ or ‘.csv’ formats. The uploaded data files will be checked against the requirements set out in the toolkit.

Benefit data

  • A data set containing the number of inhabitants aggregated by population of interest (e.g. age/gender/variant)
  • A data set containing the number of COVID-19 cases, hospital admissions, ICU admissions and deaths by population of interest aggregated by date.
  • A data set containing the number of COVID-19 cases, hospital admissions, ICU admissions and deaths by population of interest.
  • A data set containing the estimated distribution of variants of concern by population of interest
  • A data set with the coverage of the vaccine under study aggregated by population of interest.

Risk data

Three input datasets for the risk assessment are required.
  • A data set containing the number of observed events for the risk of interest aggregated by population of interest.
  • A data set with background incidence rates of the reported risk interest aggregated by population of interest.
  • A data set with the vaccine coverage aggregated by population of interest.

If available, the toolkit can calculate the required estimates up to a first booster and by variant of concern. More details on the data sources used in the analysis and the required columns and attributes can be found in the user manual. In addition, default datasets are implemented in the tool for consultation.
